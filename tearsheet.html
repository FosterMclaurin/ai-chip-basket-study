What a concentrated AI-chip bet really costs you.

Fourteen popular AI and chip stocks turned $100 into $510 over 30 months — the S&P 500 reached just $163. This tearsheet shows what you traded for that edge: roughly double the volatility, a 34% drawdown, and a third of the basket riding on semiconductors that all move together.

Growth of $100

The concentration bet paid off spectacularly — but notice how much harder the amber line falls in every drawdown. That gap between the lines is the risk premium, and the whipsaw is what you had to hold through to collect it.

Return came strapped to risk

Every name plotted by the return it delivered against the volatility you endured. The benchmarks huddle safely bottom-left; the winners live top-right, in high-volatility, deep-drawdown territory. There is no free lunch on this chart.

03

Reward for the risk you took

Raw return flatters the wildest names. The Sharpe ratio divides each name's return above cash (a 4.5% risk-free rate) by its volatility — reward per unit of risk. Ranked this way the story reorders: the diversified semi ETF beats every single chip, and one "winner" scored below cash.

Three things the data says

1.53

The smartest bet was the ETF

SMH — the VanEck semiconductor ETF — posted a 1.53 Sharpe, behind only MU and ahead of every individual chip name (NVDA, AVGO, MRVL). Diversified sector exposure delivered nearly the best reward-per-risk with far less single-stock whipsaw.

0.64 / 1.0

Five semis, but really one bet

On a 0-to-1 scale where 1.0 = moving in perfect lockstep, the five chip names (NVDA, MU, MRVL, AVGO, SMH) rise and fall together at 0.64. So owning all five isn't five separate bets — it's closer to one chip bet in five wrappers. When semis drop, they mostly drop at once.

8,750

Built to reproduce

Every figure here comes from 8,750 daily price bars — 14 tickers over ~625 trading days (2024–2026), dividend- and split-adjusted. It all regenerates from a single script, so anyone can rerun it or swap in their own tickers.

Method. Returns use dividend/split-adjusted closes. Correlations computed on daily returns, never price levels (price-level correlation is spuriously ~0.95 for any two rising stocks). The basket is daily-rebalanced across all 14 names.
Stack. yfinance (ingest) → pandas (reshape) → DuckDB (store + SQL) → numpy (stats).
